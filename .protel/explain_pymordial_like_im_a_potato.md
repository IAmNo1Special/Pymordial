## What the Heck Is This Thing?

Pymordial is **a robot that plays mobile games for you**.

That's it. That's the whole thing.

You know how you have to grind for hours in mobile games, tapping the same buttons, doing the same boring tasks over and over? Pymordial does that grinding while you sleep, eat, or actually live your life.

## The Pizza Delivery Analogy ðŸ•

Imagine you run a pizzeria, but you're tired of delivering pizzas yourself. So you hire a delivery driver.

- **BlueStacks** = Your delivery car (the Android emulator running on your PC)
- **Pymordial** = Your delivery driver (the automation brain)
- **ADB** = The steering wheel and pedals (how the driver controls the car)
- **OCR** = The driver's eyes (reading signs, addresses, and instructions)
- **Your Game** = The pizza order (what needs to be delivered/completed)

The driver (Pymordial) gets in the car (BlueStacks), uses the controls (ADB) to navigate, reads signs (OCR) to know where to go, and delivers your pizza (completes your game tasks).

**You just sit back and get paid.**

## Common Questions from Non-Techies

### "Is this cheating?"
It's automation, not hacking. You're not modifying the gameâ€”you're just automating actions you could do manually. Think of it like using a dishwasher instead of washing dishes by hand.

### "Will I get banned?"
Maybe. Games have anti-cheat systems. Pymordial tries to act human-like, but there's always risk. Use at your own discretion.

### "Do I need to be a programmer?"
To use pre-made bots? No. To build your own? Yes, you'll need some Python knowledge.

### "Does it work with [insert game]?"
Pymordial works with ANY Android game running in BlueStacks. You just need to configure it for your specific game.

### "Is it free?"
Yes, it's open source. Free as in beer AND free as in freedom.
